Curriculum Vitae (CV)

Teaching

As part of my PhD training and my job at Lund Observatory, I have the opportunity to get involved in teaching in various ways, for example, I supervise labs for students at different stages of their studies. Recently, I had the opportunity to supervise the exoplanet discovery computer lab in ASTC01 (Astrobiology) at Lund University, where I guided students to search for exoplanets through experiments using the radial velocity and transit methods. I also had the opportunity to teach a computer lab in FYSB21 (Mathematical Methods for Vibrations, Waves and Diffusion) on heat diffusion.

About Me

We investigated the effects of dropping the LTE My name is Bibiana Prinoth and I am a second year PhD student in astrophysics at Lund University, Sweden. I’m 25 years old and I finished my master’s at ETH Zürich in physics with my thesis on “Detectability of Radial Velocity Planets with ELT / METIS” in June 2020.

In September 2020, I started my PhD at Lund Observatory working on high-resolution spectroscopy of exoplanets using cross-correlation to look at the atmospheric composition of the hottest gas-giant planets in extra-solar systems.

In addition to research, I’m very interested in teaching students about the beautiful world of physics.

When not working, I enjoy writing and blogging about my life and research on a more understandable level than papers. Check out my blog, if you’re interested! Furthermore I love spending my time doing sports; I played soccer for nearly 15 years, enjoy running and like to try new sports like dancing.
